# Sample: Narrative Prose — First Person (neutral demo)

> Demonstration only. Shows the technique — interiority, behavioral emotion, clipped
> dialogue, dry undercut — on a deliberately mundane subject.

---

The deploy went out at 4:58 on a Friday, which I want on the record was not my idea.

I told myself I'd watch the dashboards for ten minutes and then go home like a person with a life. The graphs were flat and green and boring, the way you want them. I closed the laptop. I picked up my bag. I had one arm in my coat.

My phone buzzed. Then it buzzed again, in the specific way phones buzz when something is on fire and several people have noticed at once.

I didn't read the messages. I knew. You always know. I set the bag back down, slow, like putting it down gently would make the next hour any kinder, and opened the laptop on the edge of the desk standing up because sitting felt like admitting I'd be there a while.

Error rate: a wall. Not a spike — spikes come back down. A wall.

"You seeing this?" Priya, from two desks over, already had her terminal up. She hadn't put her coat on. Smart.

"Rolling back," I said.

"It's the migration."

"I know it's the migration."

"You ran the migration."

"Priya."

She held up both hands, the universal sign for *I'm just saying the true thing out loud*, and went back to her screen. Fair. I'd have said it too. I'd have said it louder.

The rollback took ninety seconds and felt like the longest part of my twenties. Then the wall came down — error rate sliding back to the flat green nothing it should have been at 4:58 — and the whole floor exhaled at once, this little collective sound, and I realized my hands were shaking and that I was, against every reasonable instinct, grinning like an idiot.

Friday. Right.
